# Crisis Escalation Triggers and Response Protocols

## Escalation Level Framework

### ðŸŸ¢ **LEVEL 1: Standard Response**
*Routine customer service issues requiring professional response*

**Response Time:** 4 hours during business hours, next business day after hours
**Authority Level:** Customer service representative or technician
**Documentation:** Standard CRM logging
**Follow-up:** 24-48 hours post-resolution

#### Triggers:
- Single customer complaint about service quality
- Scheduling request changes or conflicts
- Billing questions or minor payment issues
- General service inquiries or concerns
- Routine maintenance or warranty requests
- First-time appointment rescheduling

#### Response Protocol:
1. Acknowledge receipt within 4 hours
2. Investigate and gather information
3. Respond with solution or status update
4. Document interaction in CRM
5. Follow up to confirm satisfaction

*Serious customer concerns requiring management attention*

**Response Time:** 2 hours during business hours, 4 hours after hours
**Authority Level:** Supervisor or operations manager
**Documentation:** Incident report + CRM logging
**Follow-up:** Same day resolution confirmation

#### Triggers:
- Multiple complaints about same service issue
- Service effectiveness failures requiring re-treatment
- Customer expressing frustration or dissatisfaction
- Billing disputes over ${{BILLING_DISPUTE_THRESHOLD}}
- Equipment or vehicle issues affecting service delivery
- Weather-related service disruptions affecting multiple customers
- Staff scheduling issues requiring customer notification

#### Response Protocol:
1. Manager notification within 30 minutes
2. Personal contact with customer within 2 hours
3. Develop resolution plan with timeline
4. Implement solution with customer approval
5. Follow up same day to confirm satisfaction
6. Document lessons learned for process improvement

---

### ðŸŸ  **LEVEL 3: Urgent Crisis**
*Significant issues requiring immediate senior management intervention*

**Response Time:** 1 hour maximum, 24/7 availability
**Authority Level:** Senior management or owner
**Documentation:** Full incident report + legal review if needed
**Follow-up:** Multiple touchpoints until complete resolution

#### Triggers:
- Negative online reviews (1-2 stars) on major platforms
- Social media complaints or negative posts going viral
- Customer threats of legal action or regulatory complaints
- Property damage claims during service delivery
- Safety incidents or customer injury reports
- Service failures resulting in problem recurrence
- Multiple customer complaints about same technician or process
- Local media inquiries about company services
- Competitor mentions in customer complaints

#### Response Protocol:
1. Immediate senior management notification
2. Personal contact within 1 hour (phone call preferred)
3. Emergency response team activation if needed
4. Detailed incident investigation and documentation
5. Legal consultation if liability concerns exist
6. Comprehensive resolution plan with compensation consideration
7. Multiple follow-up contacts until customer satisfaction confirmed
8. Process review and improvement implementation

---

### ðŸ”´ **LEVEL 4: Critical Crisis**
*Severe situations threatening business reputation or requiring legal intervention*

**Response Time:** 30 minutes maximum, immediate response 24/7
**Authority Level:** Owner/CEO + legal counsel + PR specialist
**Documentation:** Legal-grade documentation + external expert consultation
**Follow-up:** Ongoing crisis management until full resolution and reputation recovery

#### Triggers:
- Multiple negative reviews posted within 24 hours
- Social media crisis with significant engagement (>100 shares/comments)
- Customer injury requiring medical attention
- Regulatory investigation or inquiry initiation
- Media investigation or negative news coverage
- Legal action filed against company
- Environmental or safety regulatory violations
- Criminal allegations involving staff or services
- Industry-wide issues affecting company reputation
- Viral negative content about company or industry
- Mass service failures affecting multiple customers simultaneously

#### Response Protocol:
1. **Immediate:** Owner/CEO notification within 15 minutes
2. **Legal:** Legal counsel contacted within 30 minutes
3. **Crisis Team:** Emergency response team assembled within 1 hour
4. **Communication:** Stakeholder notification (insurance, bonding, key partners)
5. **Public Response:** Coordinated public response within 2 hours if needed
6. **Documentation:** Legal-standard documentation of all actions
7. **Recovery:** Comprehensive reputation management and recovery plan
8. **Prevention:** Full organizational review and prevention protocol updates

- Any 1-2 star review posted

- Billing inquiries above normal volume
- Weather-related service impacts
- Staff availability issues

#### Weekly Trend Alerts (Level 1)
- Overall review score trends
- Customer satisfaction survey results
- Service completion rate changes
