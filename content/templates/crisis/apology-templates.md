## Apology Best Practices

### DO's in Apology Communications
‚úÖ Take full responsibility without excuses
‚úÖ Use specific language about what went wrong
‚úÖ Express genuine empathy for customer impact
‚úÖ Provide concrete action steps for resolution
‚úÖ Offer appropriate compensation for inconvenience
‚úÖ Include personal contact information for follow-up
‚úÖ Set realistic timelines and keep commitments
‚úÖ Follow up to ensure satisfaction with resolution

### DON'Ts in Apology Communications
‚ùå Make excuses or blame external factors
‚ùå Use vague language like "if you were upset"
‚ùå Minimize the customer's experience
‚ùå Make promises you cannot keep
‚ùå Ignore the customer's specific concerns
‚ùå Send generic, template-sounding apologies
‚ùå Fail to follow up on resolution commitments
‚ùå Admit legal liability without consultation

### Compensation Guidelines

**Minor Issues (scheduling delays, communication gaps):**
- Service discount (10-20%)
- Priority scheduling guarantee
- Direct contact access

**Moderate Issues (service quality problems, billing errors):**
- Significant service credit (25-50% of service cost)
- Free re-service or additional service
- Extended guarantees

**Major Issues (property damage, serious service failures):**
- Full service refund
- Insurance claim processing
- Multiple free services
- Management-level personal attention

**Serious Issues (safety concerns, unprofessional behavior):**
- Complete service package at no charge
- Formal process review and changes
- Long-term account management attention
- Additional compensation as appropriate
