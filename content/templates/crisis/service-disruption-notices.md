## Communication Channel Guidelines

### Email Communications
- Use for detailed explanations and formal notices
- Include all relevant contact information
- Provide multiple resolution options
- Send follow-up confirmations

### SMS Communications
- Use for immediate time-sensitive updates
- Keep under 160 characters when possible
- Include callback number
- Use for appointment confirmations

### Phone Communications
- Use for personal, immediate contact
- Document all verbal commitments
- Follow up with written confirmation
- Prioritize for upset customers

## Response Time Requirements

### Critical Disruptions (Within 30 minutes)
- Vehicle breakdowns affecting multiple appointments
- Equipment failures preventing service delivery
- Safety-related service cancellations
- Weather emergencies requiring immediate response

### Standard Disruptions (Within 2 hours)
- Single appointment rescheduling needs
- Staff availability changes
- Minor equipment issues with workarounds
- Routine schedule adjustments

### Planned Disruptions (24-48 hours advance notice)
- Holiday schedule changes
- Planned maintenance affecting service
- Routine staff schedule modifications
- Advance weather warnings

## Recovery and Follow-up Protocol

### Immediate Recovery Actions
1. Provide alternative solutions when possible
2. Offer service credits or upgrades for inconvenience
3. Ensure priority rescheduling for affected customers
4. Document all disruption communications

### Customer Satisfaction Follow-up
1. Personal call within 24 hours of resolved disruption
2. Satisfaction survey after rescheduled service completion
3. Additional follow-up if customer expresses ongoing concerns
4. Process improvement documentation for future prevention

### Internal Process Review
1. Root cause analysis of disruption event
2. Communication effectiveness evaluation
3. Customer feedback integration
4. Prevention strategy development for similar future events
