## Applying the Filter by Format

### Email Content
- Lead with the sophisticated insight
- Skip the setup/background
- Assume they know the context
- Get to the "what you might not know" quickly

### Social Posts
- Hook with the non-obvious angle
- Don't explain what the audience already knows
- Position {{PARTNER_NAME}} as peer/expert, not teacher
- Spark "I've seen that" recognition

### Blog Articles
- Can go deeper on complexity
- Still skip the basics
- Use specific examples and scenarios
- Show expertise through nuance, not breadth

## When You're Unsure

If you're not sure whether something passes the filter:

1. **Ask**: "Would a {{ROLE}} with 10+ years experience already know this?"
2. **Test**: "Does this make {{PARTNER_NAME}} sound like an expert or like they're teaching a beginner class?"
3. **Check**: "Would I forward this to a colleague, or would I feel embarrassed?"

When in doubt, cut it. It's better to be too sophisticated than to risk eye-rolls from the target audience.

---

## The Underlying Philosophy

{{PARTNER_NAME}}'s value isn't in explaining the obvious. It's in:

1. **Catching what others miss** - Hidden issues, edge cases
2. **Knowing how things actually play out** - Real-world complications
3. **Proactive protection** - Preventing problems before they happen
4. **Relationship depth** - Understanding stakeholder dynamics

Content should demonstrate these qualities, not explain basics that any professional already knows.
